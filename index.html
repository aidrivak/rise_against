History

Formation and early years

Before Rise Against, guitarist Dan Wleklinski and bassist Joe Principe were members of the Chicago punk rock band 88 Fingers Louie, but it disbanded in the late 1990's. In 1999, Principe and Wleklinski decided to form their own punk rock band called Transistor Revolt, and recruited guitarist Kevin White, drummed Tony Tintari, and lead vocalist Tim Mcllarath. In 2000, the band attracted the attention of the local punk community, as well as Fat Mike, co-founder of the independent record label Fat Wreck Chords. Fat mike offered to sign a recording contract under the condition that they would have to change their name. To which the band agreed and changed to Rise Against. After signed with Fat wreck Chords, Tintari and White left the band. Who were replaced later on in the year by drummer Brandon Barnes and guitarist Todd Mohney. In 2001, they released their debut album The Unravelling and spent the rest of the year touring. While on the tour, Wleklinski left the band. In December 2002, they band returned to the studio to work on their second album, Revolutions Per Minute that was released on April 8, 2003.

Rising popularity and International success

After the released of Revolutions Per Minute, a number of major record labels became interested, including DreamWorks Records. The band ended up signing with DreamWorks in late 2003, but DreamWorks was shortly absordeb by the Universal Music Group, and eventually folden into Geffen Records. During this period, Todd Mohney left the band and was replaced by Chris Chasse and began the recording of the new album Siren Song of the Counter Culture. The third album was released on August 10, 2004, peaking at the number 136 on the Billboard 200 album charts, gaining the band major success with the singles "Give it All", "Swing Life Away", and "life Less Frightening". After months of touring, the band re-enter the studio and recorded their fourth album, The Sufferer & the Witness that was released on July 4, 2006, peaking at number ten on the Billboard 200. In 2007 Chasse left the band and Zach Blair joined shortly thereafter as the lead guitarist. Rise Against released their fifth album Appeal to Reason on October 7, 2008, the album peaked at number three on the Billboard 200. Also, it was their first album to commercial success internationally. The album peaked in several coutries, including number seven in Australia, number twenty-one in Germany, number one in canada, number thirty-four in New Zeland, number Fifty-one in Sweden, number forty- in Switzeland, and number sixty-eight in the United kingdom. Three songs from Appeal to Reason were released as singles: "Re-Education (Throught Labor)", "Audience of One", and "Savior". All singles charted consecutive weeks spent on both the Hot Rock Songs and Modern Rock Tracks charts.

Recent years

Rise Against embarked on a North American tour with bands Rancid, Billy Talent, Killswitch Engage, and Riverboat Gamblers in June and July 2009. After touring for almost two years Rise Against had begun recording their sixth studio album for a 2011 release, at the Blasting Room in Fort Collins, Colorado. Rise Against has announced two South American shows in Brazil and Argentina and a run of European shows in late February and March 2011 respectively. Endgame was released on March 11, 2011, in the United States peaking at number two on the Billboard 200, receiving positive reviews from critics and commercial success in the United States and Canada, along with the band's three previous albums. The Black Market was released on July 15, 2014. In the United States, the album entered at number three on the Billboard 200 and sold 53,000 copies in its first week. It was their fourth consecutive album to debut within the top ten on the Billboard 200, and it spent eleven weeks on the chart. The Black Market was an international success, in particular in Canada, where it became Rise Against's third consecutive album to reach number one. On April 20, 2017, Rise Against announced the title of their new album Wolves which was released on June 9, 2017. To promote the album, the band announced a headlining North American tour in fall 2017 with Pierce the Veil and White Lung supporting. In early 2021, news of a new Rise Against album began to surface when the band began posting cryptic videos on their social media accounts. These videos eventually lead to the announcement of Nowhere Generation on March 17, 2021.
